The income curves of different career paths after Caltech¶

First post! I will explore using some salary data and crude models, several different career paths that a Caltech undergraduate can take after four years of torture:

  1. Software development job at a large software company (like Google or equivalent)
  2. Software development job at a large software company and then a startup at age 30
  3. Investment banking, consulting, quant hedge fund
  4. PhD and then go to a quant hedge fund: fake academic
  5. PhD and attempt to become a professor: true academic

Some assumptions¶

Everybody works until retirement (assume at 65), and the non-true academics will have similar eating habits and expenses for most of their life. The true academic (option 5) and the fake academic (option 4) will eat free food from campus events and Panda Express during grad school, because they are grad students. This habit will continue into later life for the true academic (option 5) because being a post-doc isnt much better. We will not look at constant career switches. Housing will be \$2500/month for the non-academics until age 30, and then become \$3500/month because they will require more space. For the academics, academic housing will be \$1200 per month, and also go to \$3500/month after age 30.

Base Salaries¶

  1. Google SWE: \$120000
  2. Google SWE \$120000 and senior SWE at Snapchat: [\$160000](https://www.glassdoor.com/Salary/Snap-Salaries-E671946.htm)
  3. Papa Goldman: \$74000, BCG: \$150000, Two Sigma: \$150000
  4. Caltech grad student \$32000 and Two Sigma quant researcher: \$175000
  5. Caltech grad student \$32000, post-doc \$50000, assistant prof \$137000, associate prof \$150000, real prof \$210000

Now we can plot some crude salary curves, without salary growth!¶

  • Cost of housing: as above
  • Cost of food for non-academics: average \$40 per day (forget Google's free food), goes up to \$60 per day after age 30 due to self+kids
  • Cost of food for academics: \$20 per day, goes up to \$35 per day after age 35 if a true academic.
  • Cost of happiness (vacation, non-essential goods) for non-true academics: \$15000 per year

ages = np.arange(22, 65+1)
wages = np.repeat([[120000,120000,74000,150000,150000,32000,32000]], len(ages), axis=0).T

# wage changes:
wages[1][(30-22):len(ages)] = 160000 # snapchat
wages[-2][(28-22):len(ages)] = 175000 # two sigma quant researcher after phd

# the true academic:
wages[-1][(28-22):(35-22)] = 50000
wages[-1][(35-22):(40-22)] = 137000
wages[-1][(40-22):(50-22)] = 150000
wages[-1][(50-22):len(ages)] = 210000

# housing costs
housing = 12*np.repeat([[2500,2500,2500,2500,2500,1200,1200]], len(ages), axis=0).T # annualize
# after age 30:
housing.T[(30-22):len(ages)] = 12*np.repeat([[3500,3500,3500,3500,3500,3500,3500]], 
                                            len(ages)-(30-22), axis=0)
# food costs
food = 365*np.repeat([[40,40,40,40,40,20,20]], len(ages), axis=0).T
food[0:-1].T[(30-22):len(ages)] = 365*np.repeat([[60,60,60,60,60,60]], 
                                            len(ages)-(30-22), axis=0)
food[-1][(35-22):len(ages)] = 365*35 # true academic food
happiness = 15000

# total
net_earnings = wages-housing-food
net_earnings[0:-1] -= happiness # only the true academic doesn't have fun
accumulated = np.cumsum(net_earnings, axis=1)
